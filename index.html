TV Energy Consumption Data Story

This website presents a data story based on an Australian television energy dataset. It aims to help consumers understand how TV brands, screen size, and energy consumption relate to each other.

1. Who is the audience?

The audience for this visualisation is Australian consumers who are planning to purchase a television. They are interested in understanding energy usage, brand options, and how screen size impacts performance.

2. What does the audience want to know?

  • Which screen technologies are most common?
  • Which TV brands dominate the market?
  • How does screen size affect energy consumption?

3. Question 1: Which screen technologies are more frequent?

This question shows which TV technologies appear most often in the dataset.

Answer

The chart shows the frequency of screen technologies such as LCD, LCD (LED), and OLED. LCD and LCD (LED) TVs are more common, while OLED TVs appear less frequently.

Main takeaway: LCD-based technologies dominate the dataset.

4. Question 2: Which TV brands are most common?

This question examines the distribution of TV brands in the dataset.

Answer

The pie chart shows that Samsung has the largest share of TV models, followed by brands like Kogan and LG. Smaller brands such as Hisense and Eko contribute less, while a large "Other" category represents many minor brands.

This indicates that the market is both dominated by a few major brands and also includes many smaller competitors.

Main takeaway: A few major brands dominate, while many smaller brands share the rest of the market.

5. Question 3: How does screen size affect energy consumption?

This question explores the relationship between screen size and energy consumption.

Answer

The scatter plot shows a strong positive relationship between screen size and energy consumption. As screen size increases, energy usage also increases significantly.

While there is some variation between TVs of the same size, the overall trend is very clear.

Main takeaway: Screen size is the main factor affecting energy consumption.

6. Final Conclusion

The analysis shows that while brands and technologies vary, screen size has the biggest impact on energy consumption. Larger TVs consume significantly more energy than smaller ones.

7. About the Data

Source: Australian TV energy dataset

Processing: Cleaned and analysed using KNIME

Limitations: Dataset may not reflect all market conditions
